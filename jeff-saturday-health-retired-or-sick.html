Is Indianapolis Colts Coach Retired Or Sick?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, May 24, 2024

NO! Jeff Saturday is neither sick nor retired. In contrast, he joined Indianapolis Colts as an interim head coach on 7 November, which has generated huge shock and criticism.

Born Jeffrey Bryant Saturday, Jeff Saturday is an NFL coach and former professional football player who played for Indianapolis Colts from 1999 to 2011. He was also an offseason and practice squad member for Green Bay Packers.

The Georgia native began his coaching career in 2017, becoming the head coach of Hebron Christian Academy (school). On 7 November 2022, Jeff Saturday was named the interim head coach of the Indianapolis Colts.

In addition to his playing and coaching career, Saturday is a former EPSN sports analyst.

Jeff Saturday Health Update: Are Indianapolis Colts Coach Retired Or Sick?

NO! Jeff Saturday is neither sick nor retired. In contrast, he joined the famous football team, Indianapolis Colts as an interim coach on 7 November.

The 47-year-old coach took one of the most significant steps in his coaching career by deciding to join the Indianapolis Colts.

Jeff Saturday was born on 18 June 1975 in Atlanta, Georgia. He went to Shamrock High School and played on the school’s football team. In one of the interviews in 2012, his high school coach, Ron Gartrell, revealed that eighty percent of their offense was behind the former center.

However, Gartrell could not sell Southeastern Conference schools on Jeff’s abilities.

The former player received a scholarship from the University of North Carolina and continued his collegiate career. From 1994 to was a four-year letterman for North Carolina Tar Heels football team.

The Baltimore Ravens signed the athlete as an undrafted free agent in 1988. But he was cut out of the team without playing a single game. Then, Jeff Saturday joined Indianapolis Colts and played 13 seasons.

He won Super Bowl XLI and was selected to four All-Pro teams and five Pro Bowls.

Jeff Saturday’s Hire Has Drawn A Lot OF Criticism

As mentioned above, Jeff Saturday has joined Indianapolis Colts as an interim coach. The former EPSN analyst replaced Frank Reich as head coach.

The news has shocked many NFL professionals. In addition, the move has also generated widespread criticism as there are several former NFL head coaches on the staff and a lack of coaching experience beyond the high school level.

Bill Cowher addressed Jeff Saturday’s hire as a disgrace to the profession. Many top-level Colts employees don’t seem to favor the move.

Amid criticism, the Indianapolis Colts owner, Jim Irsay, stood firm with the decision and said the head coach might remain beyond 2022.

Jeff made his head coaching debut against the Las Vegas Raiders, where Saturday led his team to win 25-20.

Meet Jeff Saturday Wife, Karen, And Kids

With all that said about the former offensive lineman’s professional life, let’s look at his marital life.

Jeff is happily married to Karen Saturday. The couple tied the knot over two decades ago. Mrs. Saturday is a woman of secrecy, and she isn’t active on social media. However, she occasionally gets featured on the head coach’s social media feeds.

According to The Focus, the Saturday couple has been highly active in the Indianapolis community. In 2013, the Indiana Sports Corporation presented them with the Pathfinder Award.

Jeff and his wife have also been part of several charitable organizations, including the People’s Burn Foundation, Kid’s Voice of Indiana, Riley Children’s Hospital, etc.

The married duo is doting parents of three kids – Jeffrey Douglas, Savannah Faith, and Joshua Bryant. The eldest son of the head coach is a wide receiver for the North Carolina Tarheels football team.
