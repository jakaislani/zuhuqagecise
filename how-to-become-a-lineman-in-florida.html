How much does a lineman apprentice make in Florida?

As of Jun 7, 2021, the average annual pay for a Lineman Apprentice in Florida is $41,288 an year. Just in case you need a simple salary calculator, that works out to be approximately $19.85 an hour.

How do I start a lineman career?

Most positions offered four years of paid apprenticeship on-the-job training (7,000 hours) as well as in-classroom training before becoming a Journeyman Lineman. To advance to becoming a Journeyman Lineman, it is helpful to have at least a two-year associate’s technical degree or certification from a trade school.

How hard is it to become a lineman?

It’s a great career, and yes it is very hard. You must be both physically strong, pretty sharp mentally and willing to be uncomfortable for long periods of time. It doesn’t matter if it’s 12 degrees and sleeting, or 105 with 90% humidity, the job has to get done.

Is it worth going to lineman school?

Even when you make journeyman, it’s going to be several years before you are seasoned and have a good grasp on what is going on in every type of situation. So my advice, go to the best lineman school you can afford, because this is going to be the cornerstone of your career, as you move forward.

Is lineman a dangerous job?

Utility line work is in the top 10 of the most dangerous jobs in America. Around 30 to 50 workers in every 100, 000 are killed on the job every year. Many others suffer non-fatal loss of limbs from electrical burns and mechanical trauma. That’s more than twice the fatality rate of police officers and firemen.

How many linemen die each year?

According to NTOF data, the average annual fatality rate for power line workers is 56.3 deaths per 100,000 employees.

Do linemen make good money?

An electrical lineman’s median annual income was $69,380 in May 2017, reports the Bureau of Labor Statistics; half made more, and half made less money. The bottom 10 percent of electrical linemen made under $37,600, while the top 10 percent earned very generous wages over $99,860.
